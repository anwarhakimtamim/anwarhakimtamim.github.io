About Me

Hi! My name is Anwar Hakim Tamim, and I am currently a graduate student pursuing a Master of Science in Microelectronics and Semiconductor Engineering at the University at Buffalo. I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology in Bangladesh. I have always been motivated by curiosity and the drive to understand how things work, which has shaped my academic journey and enabled me to bridge the gap between theoretical device physics and hands-on semiconductor fabrication.

My academic focus is in semiconductor process engineering, and I aspire to build a career in the semiconductor industry as a process or equipment engineer. My goal is to contribute to the design, implementation, and optimization of fabrication processes that enhance device performance, reliability, and scalability in advanced technology manufacturing.

Outside of academics, I enjoy DIY projects, whether it’s tuning up my bicycle to keep it running smoothly or repairing everyday items around the house when they break. I like the challenge of figuring out how things work, troubleshooting problems, and finding practical solutions—and arranging those solutions by making the best use of the resources available to me. In my free time, I also love playing soccer, enjoying table tennis matches, and watching superhero movies, which help me recharge and stay balanced.
